- 🥋BJJ
  - General themes
    - Vary the game: Push-pull, right-left, up-down, slow-fast
    - Don't give up head control, don't let partner grab head
    - Turn hips towards partner, not away
  - Closed guard (bottom)
    - Lapel attacks - must break posture first (push/pull, fake sweep, bring in with knee/hip pull)
      - Own lapel
        - Across partners neck, pass to opposite hand (open as opposed to crossed, grip). Hand fight when partners tries to defend. Come under and across for choke.
        - Partner defends choke:
          - Stuff hand and elevate guard trapping partners arm. Attack the other arm via straight arm lock (gable grip on top of partners elbow).
      - Partners lapel
        - Across back, first to opposite side hand. Bend upper body to reach under and through to get a strong cross grip.
          - Reach over and across for cross choke.
          - Partner defends:
            - Grab across elbow and pull arm across
              - Choke
              - Shrimp out and armbar
              - Go under and across for choke
        - If can't get across back, crush partners shoulder
    - Armbars
      - Tinocos
        - 1
          - Grab same-side sleeve
          - On the same side as the sleeve grip, foot on partners hip, raise own hip to bring own knee at the height of partners shoulder
          - Opposite leg goes in partners armpit pushing over to break posture sideways, heavy across partners back
          - Foot on hip leg, on partners shoulder - no need to go around the head, just shoulder on the same side
        - 2
          - Cross grip sleeve - pull tight
          - Other hand cross grips collar - pull tight
          - Open legs and elevate the guard. Leg on top of the shoulder of the arm under attack
          - Lock feet, then twist to turn partner down towards the arm under attack
          - Push hard on collar to open space to bring leg over the head
          - Bring heels down and towards yourself to trap the arm tight
          - Lift hips to finish
        - 3
          - Same side sleeve grip, other hand goes over and across, grabbing behind partners elbow. Bring to center line, press down
          - Open same side lapel, feed to arm trapping the arm - hold tight
          - Same side foot on hip, lift hip, push partner over with other leg (axe down across partners back/shoulders)
          - Turn to make space, and pass leg over to other side or partners head
          - Grab 
  - Side control 1-2-3
    - Side control 1
      - One knee on partners hip, the other on the shoulder. Keep near side shoulder and arm of the floor and isolated. Prevent partner from bringing elbow inside. Strong grip under partners head and behind far shoulder, or better - feed partners far lapel under far armpit and grip. Lift partner into yourself. Far side under hook.
    - Side control 2
      - Sit through facing partners head, back towards partners feet. Leg closest to partner keeps bringing partners near side arm higher. 
        - Can attack armbar on near arm (stuff between legs) 
        - Switch sides again to side control 3
        - Step over partners head with far leg and attack either far arm (triceps) or close arm arm-bar.
    - Side control 3
      - Back faces partners head. Hip scoot even higher to bring partners arm higher. Partner has no visibility on what's going on.
        - Release own far lapel and quickly pass from free hand to hand behind partners head for choke.
        - Feed own far foot over partners body for mount. Foot lands high so that kneed comes up tight behind partners shoulder/neck. Keep control of the arms for armbar attacks.

  - Instagram mobility mix
    - Zerker Jefferson
      - Weight in the crease of the arms, legs straight, bend over forwards letting the spine curl in a controlled manner. Come back up, and repeat.
    - Pancake T rotation
      - Sitting with legs straight out wide as far as possible. Kettlebell in one hand raise over head. Other hand goes forward on the floor, upper body follows hand, bending forward until folded over outstretched arm. Come back up, and repeat.
    - Elevated adductor raises
      - One leg on box, opposite side elbow on the floor, hips off the floor. Raise, the lower the other leg.
    - Deficit split squats
      - Feet on two separate boxes. Split squat down between them. Come back up, repeat.
  - Ankles
    - Band around ankle, below bone. Raise foot on box, band pulls back and down. Flex knees forward to hold. Relax, come back, then repeat.
  - Hips
    - Squat position, hold on to something in front. Press hips in between legs to open up hips.
  - Shoulders
    - Ball behind shoulder, lay on back, elbow at 90degrees, alternate bringing palm/back of hand to the floor
    - Tom Morrison
      - Thread the needle
        - On all fours, on hand goes inside and under the other. Lower same side ear towards ground. Come back up, hand towards the sky, keeping chest inline in the rotation
      - Shoulder rotations
        - On all fours, keeping elbows straight - rotate shoulders to the front, then to the back
      - Shoulder dislocate
        - With a light resistance band. Starting with the band across the waist, pull band apart and keep tension, raise arms to overhead, then come down behind the back. All the way down, switch direction and come back up. Everything done slowly and with control.
      - Dumbbell shoulder rotation
        - With dumbbell in on hand, open palm to pointing outside. Bring arm across the waist while rotating the palm inside. On the opposite hip, raise the arm and bring the dumbbell around the head. Hand comes down past the same side shoulder, open up palm to the outside again and lower to starting position. Reverse move for one full rep.

    - Early rehab
      -  Quadriceps Sets (Quad Sets)
        - Lie on your back with your operated leg straight
        - Tighten the muscle on top of your thigh, pushing the back of your knee down
        - Hold for 5-10 seconds, then relax
        - This helps maintain quad strength and control without stressing the knee joint
      - Straight Leg Raises
        - Lie on your back with your non-operated leg bent
        - Keep your operated leg straight and tighten your quad
        - Lift the straight leg about 6-12 inches off the ground
        - Lower slowly and controlled
        - This strengthens hip flexors and quads while minimizing knee stress
      - Heel Slides
        - Lie on your back with legs straight
        - Slowly slide your heel toward your buttocks, bending your knee
        - Only go as far as comfortable/allowed
        - Slide back to starting position
        - This helps regain knee flexion in a controlled manner
      - Ankle Pumps
        - Sit with your leg elevated
        - Point your toes forward, then pull them back
        - This maintains ankle mobility and helps with circulation
      - Hamstring Sets
        - Lie on your back with your knee slightly bent
        - Push your heel into the bed/floor
        - Hold for 5-10 seconds, then relax
        - This maintains hamstring strength without putting stress on the knee
    - Later rehab
      -  Progressive Single-Leg Strength Work
        - Single-leg press (starting light, progressing weight)
        - Bulgarian split squats
        - Step-ups with increasing height and load
        - Single-leg deadlifts (initially bodyweight, progressing to weighted) These target quad strength while developing proprioception
      - Balance and Proprioception Training
        - Single-leg balance on BOSU ball
        - Single-leg hops with controlled landing
        - Multi-directional lunges on unstable surfaces
        - Agility ladder drills These rebuild joint awareness and control
      - Advanced Strength Development
        - Barbell squats (once cleared, starting partial range)
        - Terminal knee extensions with resistance bands
        - Walking lunges with progressive load
        - Leg extensions with careful weight progression Focus on perfect form before increasing load
      - Sport-Specific Movement Patterns
        - Controlled agility drills
        - Progressive jumping exercises
        - Deceleration training
        - Change of direction drills These prepare you for return to sport
      - Endurance Training
        - Stationary bike intervals
        - Swimming (once wound is healed)
        - Elliptical with resistance
        - Anti-gravity treadmill running Build cardiovascular fitness while protecting the knee
