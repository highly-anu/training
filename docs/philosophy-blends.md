# Common Philosophy Blends

This guide shows how to combine philosophies to achieve specific training outcomes. These are reference templates based on proven methodology blends.

## Alpine Climbing / Mountain Performance

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Uphill Athlete** (60%) - Aerobic base, polarized training
- **Horsemen GPP** (25%) - Structural strength, durability
- **Kelly Starrett** (15%) - Mobility, joint prep

**Rationale:** Mountain endurance demands a large aerobic engine (Zone 1-2 work), load-bearing strength for pack carry, and joint durability for steep terrain.

**Phase progression:**
- Base (8w): Build aerobic foundation (80% Z1-Z2), establish structural strength (3×5 squat/deadlift), daily mobility
- Build (6w): Increase vertical gain, add pack weight, introduce threshold intervals (1-2×/week)
- Peak (4w): Sport-specific simulation (long weighted rucks), sustained threshold work, reduce strength to maintenance
- Taper (2w): 40-50% volume reduction, maintain intensity, emphasize recovery

## SOF / Tactical Performance

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Gym Jones** (40%) - Work capacity, mental toughness
- **Horsemen GPP** (30%) - General physical preparedness
- **Starting Strength** (20%) - Foundational strength
- **Uphill Athlete** (10%) - Aerobic base

**Rationale:** Tactical athletes need high work capacity across all domains, tolerance to varied stressors, and resilience under load.

---

## BJJ Competition Prep

**Philosophy mix:**
- **BJJ-specific** (50%) - Technical skill, mat time
- **Gym Jones** (25%) - Anaerobic capacity, circuits
- **Horsemen GPP** (15%) - Durability, conditioning
- **Kelly Starrett** (10%) - Injury prevention, mobility

**Rationale:** Competition BJJ demands sport-specific conditioning (repeated high-intensity efforts), durability for multiple matches, and joint protection.

---

## Ultra Endurance

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Uphill Athlete** (70%) - Polarized aerobic training
- **Kelly Starrett** (20%) - Movement durability
- **Horsemen GPP** (10%) - Structural resilience

**Rationale:** Ultra-distance events are primarily aerobic with massive time-on-feet demands. Mobility and structural durability prevent breakdown.

---

## Max Strength Focus

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Starting Strength** (70%) - Linear barbell progression
- **Horsemen GPP** (20%) - Work capacity
- **Kelly Starrett** (10%) - Joint prep, mobility

**Rationale:** Pure strength development benefits from focused linear progression with minimal interference, plus work capacity and mobility to support heavy training volume.

---

## General GPP / Fitness

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Horsemen GPP** (40%) - Balanced work capacity
- **CrossFit** (30%) - Mixed-modal conditioning
- **Starting Strength** (20%) - Strength foundation
- **Kelly Starrett** (10%) - Mobility

**Rationale:** General preparedness requires balanced development across strength, conditioning, and movement quality without specialization.

---

## Injury Rehab / Return to Training

**Philosophy mix:**
- **Kelly Starrett** (40%) - Movement restoration, joint prep
- **ATG** (30%) - Joint strengthening, bulletproofing
- **Starting Strength** (20%) - Progressive loading
- **Horsemen GPP** (10%) - Gradual work capacity rebuild

**Rationale:** Post-injury progression emphasizes movement quality, controlled loading, and gradual capacity restoration.

---

## Using These Templates

In the Program Builder:
1. Select **Philosophy Mode**
2. Choose the philosophies listed (e.g., Uphill Athlete, Horsemen GPP, Kelly Starrett)
3. Set weights approximately as shown (exact percentages will be normalized)
4. Adjust phase duration and constraints to match your schedule
5. Fine-tune priorities in the "Training Priorities" section if needed

These are starting points - adjust based on your experience level, schedule, and specific goals.
