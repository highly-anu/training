# CrossFit Journal — Extracted Notes

Sources: CrossFit Journal articles 2002–2012 (Glassman, Rippetoe, Starr, Dougherty, Takano, Vaughn)

---

## Core Programming Philosophy

1. **Functional movement primacy**: Free weights, gymnastics, metabolic conditioning over machines
2. **Intensity > volume**: Submaximal volume with supramaximal effort; "constantly varied, functional movement, high intensity"
3. **Broad GPP is protective**: Mixed modal conditioning reveals and corrects weaknesses
4. **Constant variation prevents accommodation**
5. **Progressive overload + mastery**: Form before load, benchmark testing to track progress
6. **Simplicity**: Fewest exercises producing greatest adaptations

## Equipment Philosophy

**Essential minimum:**
- Olympic barbell + bumper/rubber plates
- Pull-up bar
- Box/bench (20" primary)
- Jump rope

**High-value additions:**
- Squat rack / power rack
- Concept II rower (rated highest for metabolic conditioning)
- Gymnastics rings, parallettes
- Kettlebells
- Rope for climbing

**Intentionally excluded:** cable machines, leg press, isolation equipment

## Beginner Program (Glassman 2003)

**Structure:** 12-week, 5 days/week, <30 min/day

**Core four movements:** walk/jog/run, deadlift, push press, squat

**Weeks 1–2:** Form only (no load on runs)

**Weeks 3–12:**
- Mon/Wed/Fri: Barbell strength circuit (all three movements, timed from start to finish — track time, goal is to maintain or decrease over weeks 6–12)
- Tue/Thu: Running — multiple 400m efforts, rest = preceding run time (auto-regulates difficulty)

**Loading:**
- Deadlift: ~5 lbs/week increase
- Push Press: ~5 lbs/every 2 weeks
- Only increase when prior session completed successfully

**Progression principles:**
- Gradual volume increase: +1 lap every 2 weeks on running days
- Work:rest ratio auto-regulates as speed improves

## Squatting Standards (Rippetoe, 2006)

- Below parallel is mandatory: top of patella and iliac fold must drop below horizontal
- Femur/foot alignment: parallel throughout, no knee cave
- Toe angle: ~30 degrees outward
- Heel position: flat on floor
- Torso angle: ~45 degrees with floor
- Slight forward knee travel (1–2") is acceptable
- Half squat only loads quadriceps → disproportionate anterior knee stress; dangerous
- Posterior chain engagement (hamstrings + adductors) at full depth balances knee stress

## Strength Progression Principles (Starr articles)

**Linear progression (novice):**
- Add weight each session on major lifts
- 3 sets of 5 is primary working structure
- Deload when stalled; return to previous weight and re-progress

**Workload model (intermediate):**
- Track total workload (sets × reps × weight) over time
- Workload must progressively increase to drive adaptation
- Variation in set/rep scheme is legitimate progression tool

**The quick lifts (Starr):**
- Power clean, hang clean, power snatch as conditioning tools
- Technical barrier is real but manageable; coach form strictly
- These build explosive strength and conditioning simultaneously

---

## Olympic Lifting (Takano, 2010)

- Snatch and clean & jerk require coaching; self-taught errors compound over time
- Common beginner errors: early arm bend, early hip extension, receiving position instability
- Progressive loading is critical: technique must not degrade under load
- Competition lifts as conditioning tool: light loads, higher reps acceptable

## Key Programming Rules

- **Form mastery before advancement**: biggest mistake is teaching advanced movements to unprepared athletes
- **Rhabdo awareness**: first exposure to high-volume workouts requires scaling
- **Benchmark testing**: 3 varied examples per period, retested after training blocks
- **Nutrition**: Zone diet introduced as recovery/performance layer; fish oil supplementation
- **Recovery tracking**: sleep quality, energy stability, body composition as secondary metrics
- **Public performance posting**: fosters motivation and accountability
